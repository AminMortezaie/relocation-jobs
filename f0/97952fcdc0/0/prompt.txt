## Design direction

**Subject:** This isn't a travel/relocation consumer brand — it's a personal ATS-scraping and application-tracking engineering system, self-hosted on EC2 with 299 tests. The audience is technical (recruiters, engineers evaluating it, or Amin himself). The homepage should read like **infrastructure**, not a wanderlust travel app — that's the aesthetic risk: avoid every visa-stamp/globe/boarding-pass cliché.

**Signature element:** A live **split-flap departures board** — like an airport arrivals board — but instead of flights, each row is a company: `BERLIN · WOLT · GO · SCRAPED 6H AGO`. It ticks and flips on load. This is literally true to the product (companies flow through a scraped catalog into tracked rows) and gives one unmistakable, mechanical moment instead of a generic hero gradient.

**Token system:**
- **Color:** `#0B0E11` (near-black graphite bg), `#E8E6E1` (warm off-white text), `#FF8A00` (split-flap amber, used sparingly — status/accent only), `#1B2430` (panel/card surface), `#4FD1C5` (cool teal — used only for "live/scraping" indicators), hairline `#2A3038`
- **Type:** Display/mono — `IBM Plex Mono` (split-flap board, section labels, metrics) paired with `Inter` for body copy. No serif, no cream, no terracotta.
- **Layout:** Dense, panel-based, hairline dividers — evokes a monitoring dashboard, not a landing page. Sections are "modules" not "features."
- **Motion:** One orchestrated load sequence (the board flips into place), then restraint — hover states only, no scroll-triggered confetti.
